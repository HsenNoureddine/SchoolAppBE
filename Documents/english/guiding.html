Guiding Principles

To Every Context, a Discourse
A discourse targeting a community of scientists should not be the same as the one addressing a general audience in a non-specialized environment. The scientific text necessitates a high degree of cohesion between translated text and source text, so as not to obscure the scientific material. But a literary text does not require that kind of cohesion with the original, and one is likely to find it more committed to the spirit of the target language, where the translator/linguist would sometimes drop a term or substitute an allegory for another very different in order to convey the meaning, preserving nonetheless the tone and style adopted in the original text.
Not All that is Commonly Known is Correct
It is easy to conceive of the state the Arabic language has reached in our modern times, with this state of massive all-encompassing opening of the world, and the absolute supremacy of English in our daily lives, which irreversibly marked our culture. It is no longer possible, or rarely so, to find a media outlet not riddled with linguistic mistakes, or a book, or a text- scientific or literary- unattained by this flaw. The state of widespread linguistic mistakes has become so thoroughly ingrained and rooted that the reader became unable to tell the sound linguistic use from the flawed, and not only the reader, but also more and more frequently, the linguist.

Services

Editing
Re-read a text and highlight syntactic mistakes, but also grammatical and typographical ones, and make edits accordingly.
ProofReading
Re-read a text addressing only grammatical and typographical mistakes and make changes accordingly.
Transcription
Convert the content of any type of audio documents - in any of the aforementioned languages- into written material.
Summarizing
Capture the most important content of any written document, and reproduce an abridged, accurate, and coherent version of it.
Translation
Translate books, texts, articles, theses, papers, and speeches of any denomination .
Sworn Translation
Translate any document requiring the stamp of a sworn translator, from and into Arabic, English and French.
Reviewing
Compare a translation to the original text, highlight any discrepancies in the Translated Text, and make edits accordingly.
